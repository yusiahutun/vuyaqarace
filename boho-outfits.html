12 Key Pieces That Define a Boho Outfit

The boho revival of the mid-aughts saw stylish It Girls like Sienna Miller and The Olsens embracing head-to-toe throwback flower power looks, but today's resurgence is decidedly more subtle. Instead of the over-accessorized takes of previous eras, the slow burn of boho that's sweeping through street style is less about busting out the all-over fringe and florals and more about embracing the best of the style's key pieces. Styling is the name of the game for this new iteration on boho, and structure, proportion, and, yes, restraint are all for the best boho outfits, already perfectly executed on stars as big as Jennifer Lopez.

Feeling inspired? Keep scrolling for 12 boho outfit ideas and the key pieces to look out for.

Patchwork Denim

One of the best ways to update any trend is to revisit proportions. Whether it's patchwork denim pants, a skirt, a dress, or a jacket, simply pairing them with pieces in the latest proportions like an oversized leather jacket, bomber jacket, or winter coat provides a chic and updated look while still harking back to its boho roots.

Vests

Say "goodbye" to the fringed macramé or suede styles of the Summer of Love era. Instead, embrace a longer style in a sophisticated material like wool. A longer, belted style brings boho ease to everything from slacks to shorts and gets an extra style boost from motorcycle boots.

Crop Tops

Crop tops have a place outside of the music festival, and the best way to wear them now is in a streamlined monochromatic look. Fabric choice is key, and a knit set with a high waist remains sleek while holding onto its boho roots with the addition of fringe and chunky flatforms.

Shearling

Nothing screams Penny Lane fangirl like a full shearling maxi-coat, but an asymmetrical bomber jacket trimmed in shearling, or even an unexpected piece like shorts constructed in shearling put a truly modern twist on the classic boho fabric. Lug-sole boots, loafers with socks, or sporty sneakers keep the look cool and finished.

Puff-Sleeve Tops

The puff-sleeve top is only as fashionable as what it's paired with, and keeping it simple with a pair of high-waisted pants (especially jeans) is critical and helps balance the volume of a puffy top. Details like a high neck or gathered wrist and keeping accessories simple allow the boho staple to shine and keep it modern.

Statement Coats

Whether it's plaid, knit, patchwork, or floral, the key to updating a boho statement coat is to let it shine. Instead of mixing it with more patterns, isolate it with a head-to-toe denim or monochromatic look. Allow the coat to be your pop of color, whether what you're wearing underneath is casual or dressier office wear.

Macramé

Few materials are as polarizing as macramé, but in the right hands, it can wink to its boho origins while taking on an energy all on its own. Try pairing a macramé cami with a black midi skirt or a macramé skirt with a crisp white button-down — a contrast that makes the look unexpected and fully modern. If you're brave enough to try a head-to-toe look, try adding classic and sleek accessories like a black belt, slingbacks, and a structured handbag.

Culottes

Culottes and high boots are a perpetual match made in heaven, but the best way to make the classic boho pairing sing is to add a structured top to the mix. Contrasting shades can lend a chic colorblocking effect, but keeping it monochromatic and letting the boots pop is also lovely.

Floral Maxis

It's easy for a beloved boho staple like the floral maxi to take on a "permanent vacation" look, but pairing the dress with sporty sneakers, a micro-handbag, and minimal accessories keeps it fresh and breezy.

Fringe Shawls

An unexpected styling twist can bring new life to even the most surprising of pieces, and a fringe shawl worn over a super crisp white maxi dress is just the kind of contrast that makes boho stick around. Consider throwing a fringe shawl over a wool coat, tied at the waist over jeans with a motorcycle jacket, or around the strap of a leather bag.

Floral Slip Dresses

This boho staple saw a resurgence in the '90s and is making its return now as a key layering piece. To get maximum use (and maximum appeal), try wearing a slip dress over a turtleneck or sweater, and eschew tights for cozy knit socks. A puffy vest, strappy sandals, or chunky boots finish the look with modern flair.

Floral Quilting

Scale matters and an oversized quilted look like a coat or sweater can look costume-y or dated if they're not paired with the right pieces. Wide-leg jeans, classic white sneakers, and a small bag complements the boho energy of a heavily quilted look while giving off cool energy.
