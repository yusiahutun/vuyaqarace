Murder suspect Willie Parker confronted by Kristin Ziade after police standoff

A tearful widow rushed from her husband's funeral Thursday to the scene of a standoff where police and FBI agents sought to apprehend the man accused of killing him in Kansas City, Kan.

Authorities arrested a man believed to be Willie E. Parker near the 2700 block of Highland Avenue after a five-hour standoff. 

He was charged with first-degree murder in the shooting death of his employer, Michel Ziade.

His distraught wife, Kristin Ziade, recognized the man as he was being arrested and shouted, 'You killed my husband.'

Standoff: Kristin Ziade said that she wasn't leaving the scene of the standoff (photo above) until she saw her husband's suspected killer arrested

Brutally murdered: Michel Ziade (above left) was killed near the University of Kansas Hospital in Kansas City, Kan., on July 28. He leaves behind his wife, Kristin, and young daughter, Talia (photo above)

Tragic: Kristin Ziade (above right) rushed from the funeral of her husband (above left) on Thursday to the scene of a standoff where police sought to apprehend the man accused of killing him

The widow was contacted earlier about the situation and was accompanied to the hectic scene by her mother and other relatives, some of whom were still dressed in black. 

'I wanted him to know I was here,' she said told the Kansas City Star. 

'It will never bring back my husband, but at least I know he has been caught.' 

The 54-year-old man was killed in broad daylight near the University of Kansas Hospital in Kansas City, Kan. on July 28.

Caught: Authorities finally arrested Willie E. Parker, the man suspected of killing 54-year-old Michel Ziade, after a five-hour standoff

Suspected Killer: Authorities arrested a man believed to be Willie E. Parker (above) after a five-hour standoff on Thursday. He was charged with first-degree murder in the shooting death of Michel Ziade

Parker was an employee of First Class Medical Transportation Inc., which is the company the couple owned together.

Kristin Ziade told WDAF that her husband loaned Parker $1,500 two weeks prior to the murder. 

The two reportedly had an argument outside of a medical clinic at hospital before the businessman was killed. 

One of Parker's relatives said that his mental health has been declining for the past seven years, WDAF reported.

Acting on a tip, FBI agents with a fugitive task force and police from Kansas City, Mo. and Kansas City, Kan., surrounded a church that Parker was in at about 1 pm. 

The suspected killer finally exited the house and surrendered at about 5:30 p.m. after law enforcement negotiators spoke with him for hours using a megaphone. 

FBI spokeswoman Bridget Patton told the Kansas City Star that the man arrested matched Parker's description and had acknowledged to police that he was the man they were seeking. 

She said his finger prints will be used to establish his identity. 

Brutally murdered: Michel Ziade (above) owned First Class Medical Transportation Inc. with his wife. The suspected murderer, Willie E. Parker, was employed by the company

Happier Times: Michel Ziade (above left) is seen in a photo holding the hand of his young daughter, Talia (above right)

The murder victim's wife told KSHB that she wasn't leaving the standoff until Parker was arrested.

'My husband will never walk my daughter down the aisle and he will never sit next to me on this couch again,' she stated.

'I don't understand what reason or why he decided to do that that day.'

She added that she wants the suspected killer to know what he did to her family.

All smiles: Michel Ziade (above right) was described as an 'amazing father' to his only child Talia (above left) by his wife

'I'm not sure how much of an impact it will have on him, but he will have a long process,' she told KSHB.

'He'll have trial, he'll have court and I will be there everyday.'

An educational fund for the couple's five-year-old daughter Talia has been established by the family.
