The Origins of Hednesford Town FC

Hednesford Town Football Club, commonly referred to as “The Pitmen,” was founded in 1890 in the town of Hednesford, Staffordshire, England. From its humble beginnings as a small local team, Hednesford Town FC has grown to become one of the most recognizable and respected non-league football clubs in the country.

The Club’s Colours and Crest

Hednesford Town FC’s traditional colors are amber and black. The team proudly dons striped jerseys and black shorts during matches. The club’s crest features a miner’s lamp symbolizing the town’s rich mining history, along with the club’s name and year of establishment.

A Long-Standing Rivalry

Hednesford Town FC has a fierce rivalry with nearby team Stafford Rangers FC. Matches between the two sides, known as the “Staffordshire Derby,” always attract a passionate crowd and add an extra level of intensity to the proceedings.
