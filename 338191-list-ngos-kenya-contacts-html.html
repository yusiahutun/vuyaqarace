Top 15 NGOs in Kenya 2024 and their contacts: Best registered NGOs

Non-Governmental Organizations (NGOs) are typically non-profit organizations that operate independently of any government and whose purposes are to address social or political issues. They are either organized at local, national, or international levels and driven by common interests. These foundations deal with human rights, advocacy, health, and environmental issues. What are the top NGOs in Kenya, and what do they deal with?

How many NGOs are there in Kenya? The country has over 10,000 NGOs. Many of these bodies have fulfilled all NGO registration requirements, while others have not.

The top NGOs in Kenya and their contacts

Here is a list of the top registered NGOs in Kenya.

1. Africa Harvest Biotech Foundation International

Africa Harvest is one of the top American NGOs in Kenya. Its regional offices are in Johannesburg, South Africa, and Washington DC, while the country head office is located in Kenya's capital city. The NGO has ongoing projects in six African states: Kenya, Tanzania, Burundi, Nigeria, Burkina Faso, and Ghana. Just as its name suggests, the organization aims to free Africa from poverty, hunger, and malnutrition.

7. Kenya Human Rights Commission (KHRC)

KHRC is one of the top human RIGHTS NGOs in Kenya. It addresses the sustainability and effectiveness of the Kenyan human rights sector. The body also tries to deal with the liberation of minority groups and organizations, social justice, accountability, and gender mainstreaming.

Since its formation, the foundation has been recording, monitoring, and publicizing political, social-cultural, and civil rights violations. KHRC has a well-defined roadmap of achieving human rights and freedoms in Kenya. Based on a six-point action plan, its notable emphasis includes the realization of gender equality and de-ethicist politics in the country.

12. Rhino Ark

Rhino Ark is one of the numerous UK non-governmental organizations in Kenya. It is registered in Kenya, the United States, and the United Kingdom. This is the foundation that responded to Kenya's grave crisis facing the Black Rhino population in the Aberdare National Park.

Its main aim was to build a fence around the park to keep poachers away and to bar animals from invading the neighbouring community's farms.

The foundation also seeks to conserve indigenous forests, create a harmonious environment between people and wildlife. It is also keen on developing initiatives to protect the natural habitats for future generations by preventing illegal encroachment in forests.

13. Fauna & Flora International

Fauna & Flora International is an international non-profit wildlife conservation organization. It was founded in 1903 by a group of American and British naturalists. Its main goal is to safeguard Africa's mammal population, which, at the time, was declining at an alarming rate due to illegal hunting.

The organization was instrumental in establishing national parks such as Serengeti and Kruger in Tanzania and South Africa, respectively. Today, the NGO is dedicated to conserving biodiversity through building local capacity, directly protecting habitats and species, securing conservation land, and bridging the gap between business and biodiversity.

How many NGOs are there in Kenya?

The list of NGOs in Kenya is quite extensive. For example, in the financial year 2019/2020, there were 11,624 NGOs in Kenya. Of these, slightly over 9,000 were active.

How many NGOs are there in Nairobi?

In the 2019/2020 financial year, 1,143 NGOs in Nairobi were reported to have been active. Kisumu followed this with 397 active NGOs and Kiambu with 342.
