Okoa Chap Chap: Safaricom Introduces Service Allowing Customers to Complete Calls, Pay Later

  • Safaricom PLC rolled out a new airtime overdraft under the Okoa service for prepaid subscribers
  • The Okoa Chap Chap service allows Safaricom customers to complete calls when they run out of funds and pay later
  • The telecommunication firm noted that the overdraft service allows up to a maximum of KSh 10 per call

Safaricom voice service customers can now complete calls when they run out of airtime in the middle of the conversation.

The telecommunication firm rolled out a new overdraft service that allows customers an additional KSh 10 per call.

How Safaricom Okoa Chap Chap works

The company said the service, dubbed Okoa Chap Chap, gives customers with limited airtime an option to choose via an IVR prompt at the beginning of a call.

"Okoa Chap Chap overdraft facility is a feature that allows you to complete your calls when you have insufficient funds at the start of a call. You will receive an IVR prompt that will require consent to use Okoa Overdraft to complete the call," Safaricom said in an exclusive response to TUKO.co.ke.

The telco noted that once a customer accepts the complete call, the system sends a notification of the cost based on duration.

The amount charged includes a 10% service fee, common to other Okoa services.

"The same amount will be posted to the customer account as an Okoa debt with a five-day repayment period. Every Overdraft call will be limited to a maximum of KSh 10 per call," the telco added.

Safaricom charges an advance fee of 10% on Okoa services, meaning a customer receives the requested Credit Advance less 10%.

The service can be accessed by dialling USSD code *126# and following the prompts.

Safaricom Lipa na Data

This followed Safaricom Lipa na Data, a new payment feature allowing customers to pay for goods and services using mobile data bundles.

The telecommunication firm introduced the feature under the USSD code *544*34#, giving customers an option to check their eligibility, bundles to redeem and value.

A customer with 15.4GB of mobile data can redeem to get KSh 1,000 for shopping or any other bill.
