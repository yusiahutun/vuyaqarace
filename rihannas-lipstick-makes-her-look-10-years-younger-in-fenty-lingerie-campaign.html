Rihanna's lipstick makes her 'look 10 years younger' in Fenty lingerie campaign

Rihanna fans can’t get enough of her as she poses in hot pink lingerie for her underwear brand’s Valentine’s 2024 collection.

Since launching her celebrity Fenty brand seven years ago in 2017 (starting with the makeup line Fenty Beauty) Rihanna has become a well-rounded businesswoman. Makeup, skincare, fragrance, and lingerie! No wonder there hasn’t been an album from the music star since 2016. But Ms Fenty has gotten fans hot under the collar once again with photos shared from the shoot of the Valentine’s collection from her Savage X Fenty line.

Rihanna’s Valentine’s lingerie collection photoshoot

New pictures show the Umbrella singer in a matching set of hot pink underwear pouting seductively for the camera and lounging on a velvety-looking purple seat. Another picture simply shows a closeup of Rihanna.

She matched her lipstick and nails for the occasion. The lip color she sports for the campaign is a limited edition shade called Pink Limo’scene, while her nails are a slightly lighter shade of pink and lean more toward a baby pink hue.

They captioned the various Instagram posts with teasing lines such as “And that’s how the bad gal does VDay [kiss mark emoji]” and “@badgalriri giving us alllllll the love we need this VDay [heart with arrow emoji].”

Fans are obsessed and say pics could be ‘from 2006’

People couldn’t help but gush about Rih Rhi in the comments of the Savage X Fenty account posts.

“Legit thought this was Ri when she first came on the scene, she’s ageless and flawless [star eyes emoji],” wrote one person.

Another said: “Lipstick makes her look 10 years younger somehow! v playful. love it.”

“Omg, this could be from 2006???? What kind of witchcraft @badgalriri,” penned a third.

It wasn’t just Instagram, either, fans on X (formerly Twitter) were equally infatuated with Rihanna’s look.

Rihanna discusses why she founded Savage X Fenty

In an interview with Extra TV in 2021 the 35-year-old said her line was about feeling “represented and sexy.”

She said. “I want to feel sexy and I want to feel represented. I want to feel included. I want to feel like I don’t want to feel intimidated by what’s on the rack or what’s the score… I want to know that there’s something for me, something for my body,” she explained.

“There’s something that was going to work for me. I think everyone has that perspective, so I don’t want to deny anyone the experience to feel sexy or empowered. That’s the Savage brand, and it’s going to be our brand forever.”
