Man stabbed to death outside football ground among two knifed within hours in seaside town

The man who died was among two stabbings that took place within a mile of each other in Southend, Essex, on Thursday night.

Essex Police said at this early stage the two stabbings are not being linked despite the distance between them.

Police and ambulance crew were called to reports of a stabbing in Roots Hall Drive, Southend, shortly before midnight on Thursday.

The man was found with serious injuries at an address near Roots Hall stadium, the home ground of Southend United Football Club.

The victim, a man in his 30s, was rushed to hospital with a stab wound to his leg.

He later died in hospital.

Detectives are investigating the circumstances of the incident.

In a tweet, Essex Police said: We've launched a murder investigation after the death of a man at an address on Roots Hall Drive, Southend.

“A man aged in his 30s was taken to hospital with a stab wound, where sadly he later died.

“Enquiries are ongoing. Any info call South Major Crime Team on 101 or Crimestoppers UK.”

Just hours earlier, a man in his 20s was attacked by a group of men riding push bikes in Southend High Street at around 8.15pm.

The attackers fled the scene on their bikes in the direction of Park Street, police said.

The victim was taken to hospital, where he was treated for non-serious injuries.

Anyone with information about either incident has been asked to contact the South Major Crime Team on 101 or email scd.appeals.essex@essex.pnn.police.uk.
