8 Old House Renovation Blogs You Should be Reading Best Home Renovation Blogs

Vivacious Victorian

Courtesy of Vivacious Victorian

Amy and Doug Heavilin might be the ultimate DIYers. At the very least, they're are a living, breathing example of how restoring an old house as a couple can actually strengthen a marriage. Whether reupholstering furniture, stripping wallpaper or designing massive period-appropriate built-ins, they share with intimate (and often hilarious) detail how they're fixing up their gorgeous Indiana home all by themselves.

Our Favorite Quote:

(Amy recounting the first time she ever laid eyes on the home's majestic staircase)

We went up on the porch, and I looked in the left front window, while Doug was checking out damage on the porch. What I saw made me gasp. I was literally breathless and speechless.

When Doug tells the story, this is where he says, "I knew right then it was going to be ours. There was no going back. It didn't matter what was behind, above, or under that stairwell. As soon as she gasped, the rest of it didn't matter. We would fix it. But that gasp told me it was ours."

Read more at Vivacious Victorian.

The Danville Experience

Courtesy of The Danville Experience

The Danville Experience follows the trials and tribulations of a pediatric nurse practitioner and a corporate attorney as they restore a massive Gothic home that is part of Danville, Virginia's "Millionaire's Row." The before-and-after shots are absolutely exquisite—picture gigantic stained glass windows, soaring decorated ceilings and the kind of original woodwork most old house lovers only dream about. Careful: This one's addictive.

Our Favorite Quote:

As we are quickly approaching the fifteenth year of our five-year project, the question I hear most often from friends, neighbors, and curious onlookers is: "So...when will you be finished?" I used to do a brief calculation in my head of the projects already in process multiplied by those yet to be completed and factoring in an error ratio of ten percent give or take, and would reply with an approximate completion date, confident in my answer.

Really!

I actually thought that I KNEW when we would be done with the house. It was hysterical!


Read more at The Danville Experience.

Victoria Elizabeth Barnes

Courtesy of Victoria Elizabeth Barnes

Our years spent following the exploits of Victoria Elizabeth Barnes have taught us one simple truth, which is, to quote Victoria herself, "You NEVER KNOW what is on the other end of a Craigslist ad." The author and her husband keep busy filling their 1890 Victorian home with fantastic flea market, auction and Craigslist scores we could only dream of landing. Which is why we're living vicariously through their hilarious restoration and decorating adventures.

Our Favorite Quote:

Giant Fancy Things… cheaper than therapy.

Read more at Victoria Elizabeth Barnes.

Restoring Ross

Courtesy of RestoringRoss.com

In March of 2014, Ross MacTaggert did something that most people would have considered crazy: He purchased a 9,000-square-foot Victorian house in Emporia, Kansas and the adjacent (also enormous) carriage house with the intention of restoring them both back to their original splendor. Ross admits to being "awestruck and mesmerized by the huge structure... [with] a desperate, aching yearning to be a part of its resurrection." He's been chipping way at the project ever since, and chronicles his adventures in the most inspiring way on his blog, Restoring Ross.

Our Favorite Quote:

Most disturbing and altogether terrifying, the house needed a ton of work. However, a-ton-of-work can in no way truly convey the reality of just how much work was required. The projected expense and time required to even make the house livable (much less fully restored) was, yes, terrifying. Terrifying on a profound, shocking, alarming, lock-me-away level.

Read more at Restoring Ross.

Chateau de Gudanes Restoration

Courtesy of Chateau de Gudanes Restoration

You might have been cool in high school. You might even still consider yourself to be cool now. But no matter how cool you are, you aren't anywhere near as cool as this Australian family, who bought a larger-than-life chateau in the French Pyrénées that they are currently in the process of restoring. The website features hundreds of photos of the crumbling palace that'll make you giddy with delight, if not painfully jealous. Now THAT is living the dream.

Our Favorite Quote:

Hiding within these walls reside the secrets held by families for the last 300 years. They whisper colors and textures of the past. They tell us the growing heights of the children that played in these rooms. They give us glimpses back in time to the romantic sorts of events that would have been held.

Read more at Chateau de Gudanes Restoration.

Manhattan Nest

Courtesy of Manhattan Nest

Daniel Kanter started blogging in 2010, while living in a college dorm in Manhattan. Three years later, armed with exquisite taste and a knack for seeing the potential under vinyl siding, he found himself the proud owner of an 1865 fixer-upper in Kingston, New York—and you'll get to peek inside if you read his blog, Manhattan Nest. It's long been a favorite of ours, and will inspire anyone looking to flee the city in favor of small-town, old-house livin'.

Our Favorite Quote:

This week, my house feels like it is getting torn to shreds, and the copious amounts of dust and disorder that I haven't experienced since demo'ing a couple plaster ceilings a few years ago is back with a vengeance. I couldn't be happier about it. Progress once again. Feels good.


Read more at Manhattan Nest.

My Queen Anne

Country of My Queen Anne

If you're like most people, the thought of hanging wallpaper gives you a minor case of hives. One look at Rachael Meyerink's perfectly-patterned, finished dining room might change that. It's just one of many beautiful projects she's undertaken inside the 1889 Victorian home—a National Historic Landmark—that she's restoring with her family in Sioux Falls, South Dakota.

Our Favorite Quote:

In November, I emailed my husband the real estate listing for this house. I did not do this with the intention of actually pursuing its purchase. Emailing him listings for old houses is just something I DO. Living in an old house has been a dream for as long as I can remember. I love anything that is old and has a story behind it. If I had drawn a picture of my childhood "dream house," it would have looked like this house. But buy it? No.

Once my husband and I stepped inside the house, it was over. We purchased the house a week before Christmas and will move in very soon.


Read more at My Queen Anne.

A Farmhouse Reborn

Courtesy of A Farmhouse Reborn

You might recognize this handy twosome from a March 2014 episode of House Hunters, on which they found the circa 1850 brick home they're currently restoring in Charlottesville, Virginia. David and Lynne Sade live out their idyllic life on 5 acres, and share their adventures along the way on their comprehensive blog, A Farmhouse Reborn.

Our Favorite Quote:

Poor husband didn't know there was more than one shade of yellow. (I'm going to blow his mind later in life when we start to figure out a good white paint color for the master bedroom.)

So when I decided I wanted to paint our family room yellow, he just sort of assumed we'd head to the paint shop and pick up a gallon or two of yellow.

Ha.

Like it was that easy.

Read more at A Farmhouse Reborn.
