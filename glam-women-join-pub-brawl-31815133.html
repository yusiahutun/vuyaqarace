Glam women join pub brawl in packed bar on New Years Eve as punters left bloodied

A pub brawl which erupted at a bar on New Year's Eve has left its fighters bloodied and bruised, with the scrappy encounter recorded.

The shocking scenes at the Stoke-on-Trent pub were uploaded to Twitter/X, where revellers were seen smashing into one another. It was a far cry from the usual civil nature of ringing in the new year, and violence erupted throughout the put on Sunday night (December 31).

Two glam women were seen heading towards the fight, one dressed in white attire, and attempting to involve themselves in the brawl between a number of men throwing hands.

A Staffordshire Police spokesperson said: "We were called to the Green Star pub on Esperanto Way, Smallthorne, Stoke-on-Trent, at 10.15pm on 31 December to reports of a large fight.

"Officers attended are carrying out enquiries to identify those involved." They later confirmed to The Sun that officers raced to the pub to prevent the brawl from spilling over any further.

No arrests were made at the scene of the scuffle though video footage of the fight showed people leaping out of the way and trying to make their way away from the fight.

Punters were seen leaping away from the men who were throwing furniture and the Christmas tree at one another, leaving the place a bloodied mess of chairs and cutlery.

Twitter/X users were elated by the brawl, praising the camera work for showing the thugs batter into each other. One user wrote: "Some quality camera work there for once too!"

They later wrote: "Get it in landscape and it has the gritty realism of the early Bourne movies!"
