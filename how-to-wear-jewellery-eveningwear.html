How to wear jewellery for eveningwear outfits

Nothing can add a lift to your outfit – or your mood – quite like a beautiful piece of jewellery.

To demonstrate, we've teamed up with luxury British brand William & Son whose MYA Collection (which stands for Million Years Ago) can add instant allure to your evening wear.

Here, Stephanie Peers of The Style Memo models an array of dresses with the jewellery that suits them best...

1. The Cinderella gown

When it comes to an ultra-glamorous night out, only a sweeping ballgown will do. Step forward this Dior dress, which makes the perfect canvas for bright gems.

JAMIE MCGREGOR SMITH

If you've never considered yourself one for colourful stones, you might be surprised by how an emerald green necklace or earrings can lift your outfit. Try a variety of different shades to discover what suits you and will bring out the best in your colouring.

You can't go wrong with William & Son's diamond and white gold emerald necklace. As for what to wear it with, the trick is to stay within the same colour family and jewel tones, like the midnight blue of this Dior dress, are a classic.

JAMIE MCGREGOR SMITH

Stephanie wears the matching MYA collection earrings, which climb the ear and lend a refreshing twist on classic drop earrings. For the perfect finishing touch, we added a slither of silver with Jimmy Choo heels. The unexpected pop of shine, which peeps out from beneath the tulle, is ideal for modern-day Cinderellas.

SHOP THE DETAILS: Emerald & diamond necklace, POA; Emerald & diamond earrings, £31,195; Emerald & diamond ring, £21,400—all from William & Son's MYA Collection

2. The plunging neckline

Gowns with deep V necklines are de rigueur on the black tie scene and make a show-stopping statement. The question is, how to avoid going overboard?

When taking the plunge, it's all about finding the right colour and fabric, as well as hemline, to balance out the revealing neckline. The soft velvet of this Dior dress and the floor-sweeping length create a wonderfully elegant after-dark look.

JAMIE MCGREGOR SMITH

Fine jewellery is part and parcel of nailing an extreme neckline—just stick to the less-is-more philosophy when your décolletage is on show. Instead of gobstopper-size stones and heavy necklaces that can detract from the silhouette, keep accessories to a minimum.

A pair of cluster-climbing diamond earrings are the epitome of understated elegance and can add just the right amount of sparkle. And when you don't have a black-tie affair to attend, they'll look just as striking with a pair of jeans and a T-shirt as with an evening gown.

Subtly complement the earrings with delicate diamond bracelets like William & Son's pear diamond tennis bracelet, which looks great when layered and one emerald ring provides an intriguing pop of colour.

To pack a final punch, accessorise with a textured clutch (mixing fabrics is big news for AW17) and a pair of green velvet mules to match the ring—we adore this pair by Malone Soulier. You'll be turning heads all night long.

JAMIE MCGREGOR SMITH

SHOP THE DETAILS: Diamond bracelet, £41,250; Diamond ring, £10,740; Diamond earrings, £9,740; Emerald & diamond ring, £21,400—all from William & Son's MYA Collection; Gala collection Mini Bruton clutch, £3,09, William & Son.

3. The cold shoulder

The off-the-shoulder trend shows no sign of waning this season. Admittedly, a cold shoulder can be challenging to accessorise when there's so much bare skin on show, but with the right jewellery, you can achieve a fashion-forward look that doesn't feel flashy.

JAMIE MCGREGOR SMITH

The cut of this Jonathan Simkhai dress will leave your décolletage open to experimentation. William & Son's emerald and diamond necklace is perfect for filling that space. Taking centre stage, it looks so romantic against the skin (coloured stones look especially great with a tan).

To keep the focus on the necklace, avoid going overboard with the rest of your accessories, as well as your beauty look. For a modern and fresh take, keep the overall silhouette in mind. Since there is more skin on show, you want to stick with tousled waves and barely-there make-up. Cocktail party, here you come...

JAMIE MCGREGOR SMITH
