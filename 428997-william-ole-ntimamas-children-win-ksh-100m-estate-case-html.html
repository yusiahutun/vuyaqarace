William Ole Ntimama's Children Win KSh 100m Estate Case Against Brother-In-Law

  • William Ole Ntimama died on September 1, 2016
  • He left the management of his property under his wife Dorcas Pedelai who died in June 2021
  • Pedelai administered the estate jointly with her daughter Vivian Talasha until her death in January 2020
  • After her demise, her husband Eric Kimani sought control of her share

Children of the late KANU era Cabinet Minister William Ole Ntimama have won a KSh 100 million Britam investment fund case against their brother-in-law.

On Friday, October 8, Justice Alfred Mabeya ruled that the fund belonged to Ntimama's widow Dorcas Pedelai who died in June.

Mabeya delivered the judgment on behalf of Justice Francis Tuiyott, Daily Nation reported.

Feud

Pedelai ran the account with her daughter Vivian Talash Ntimama until her death in January 2020.

After her demise, her husband Eric Kimani sought control of her share, The Standard reported.

In his will, Ntimama had left two parcels of land in Lavington and another in CIS Mara Olopito under his wife's administration.

Pedelai reverted the property to Vivian, who would, in turn, take care of her.

In their petition through lawyer Omwanza Ombati, Ntimama's children argued that their sister, Vivian, was just a signatory and not the fund's owner.

Britam moved to court to seek a solution. Justice Tuiyot confirmed Vivian filled the account form but noted it was silent on how it would be divided if their mother died.

"The court, therefore, takes it that, without more, the place to start is that Mama Dorcas is the sole owner of the monies in the bank court," the judge stated.

He ruled the surviving members of the family would manage the fund.

Nginyo's widow, children in trouble

In a related story, the late businessman Lawrence Nginyo Kariuki's widow and her children found themselves in trouble for taking over the late tycoon's property without permission.

Margaret Wangari Nginyo and some of her children named Jane Alice Wambui Kiragu and Silas Macharia Kariuki are fighting to control a KSh 4 billion estate he left behind.

The High Court ruled that they withdrew over KSh70 million from his bank accounts at I&M Bank and Consolidated Bank of Kenya, respectively, before being given the go-ahead to manage it.

Kariuki lost his life on February 24, 2020. Besides a vast estate, he left behind huge bank deposits and government bonds.
