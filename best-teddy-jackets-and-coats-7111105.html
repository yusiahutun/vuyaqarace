The 13 Best Teddy Coats of 2024

I’m pretty proud of the types of coats that reside in my closet. Over the years, I’ve added a trench coat, cozy puffers, a leather bomber, and about four different teddy jackets. My penchant comes from the topper’s versatility — both my leggings and pantsuit look comfortable yet put-together when paired with a teddy coat. Stylist Alison Bruhn agrees that they’re “having a moment right now,” with more colors, lengths, and styles readily available at a variety of price points. Styles made from shearling, the traditional teddy coat material, tend to be expensive; however, those on a budget have alternative materials to consider.

Aside from being trendy, they’re also inter-seasonal with the ability to keep you cozy from fall until early spring, with layering options depending on the temperature. And when they look as cute as our favorite options do, you’ll want to get as much wear out of them as you can.

Best Overall

Ugg Frankie Sherpa Trucker Jacket

Ugg

What We Love: The pockets are fleece-lined to keep your hands warm.

What We Don't Love: Because of how deceivingly lightweight it is, it will require some layering to keep you warm.

Ugg is no stranger to creating soft and plush apparel, and this teddy jacket comes prepared to keep you cozy through the cold weather. Lined with fleece, the pockets will keep your hands toasty (in case you ever forget your gloves), and the two chest pockets offer a bit more storage opportunities for a folded mask or apartment key when you're running out for a quick errand — the side pockets don't appear to have any closure to them, so things might slip out, making the chest pockets significantly more useful.

It might not be as warm as your typical cold-weather coat, though; Ugg confirmed that the jacket actually feels lightweight because it's intended for chilly spring or fall days. Thankfully, the oversized cut lets you layer sweaters underneath, so you can easily get away with wearing it in the winter without feeling too cold.

Material: Polyester, elastane | Size: XXS-3XL | Colors: 5

What to Keep in Mind

  • Shearling vs. Teddy: Shearling and teddy are not one and the same. To begin, one describes a material and another a texture. Shearling is made of real fur, typically from a lamb, that has only been shorn (removed of fur) once. Teddy refers to the fluffy texture and can be made of both real or faux fur. Therefore, while shearling can be considered teddy, a teddy coat is not always shearling. 
  • Material: Teddy can be constructed using real fur, cotton, or synthetic fibers, like polyester and acrylic. Faux fur made of the latter three options mimics the sheep-like fluff and is intended to work as an alternative to animal products. Cotton has a natural softness and comfort to it, so jackets that have an interior lining of teddy typically opt for this fabric. However, polyester functions better as an outerwear for its strong, rip-resistant, and quick-drying properties. You’ll notice that most of the options listed here, therefore, are made of polyester.
  • Cut: Once you’ve decided on what type of texture and material you want your teddy jacket to be in, next you’ll have to decide the cut of the jacket. Are you looking for a long coat or something cropped? If you’re unsure and simply want something you can wear every day, a hem stopping just below your hips is the way to go.
  • Closure: Your safest bet is opting for a zip closure if warmth is what you’re after. That being said, there’s no denying that a single button closure looks more appropriate on a long coat. And while we love a belt closure, it can run the risk of looking like a bathrobe (although a very chic one). Still, this option allows you to fully wrap your body in the coat as snug as you want before closing it.

Your Questions, Answered

How do you style teddy coats?

Each teddy jacket comes with its own cut and style, which will ultimately require a different styling guide. But overall, the fuzzy look can be dressed up and down—or worn like a throw-and-go shacket—making it a versatile piece in your wardrobe. For a casual everyday look, “I love a teddy jacket over a structured collared blouse and some classic denim,” says fashion and beauty influencer Rosey Blair. For a more elevated take, “I would style a teddy jacket with a long sleeve knit dress and cowboy boots,” says stylist Ashleigh Ashton.

Are teddy coats warm?

The simple answer to this is, it depends.What makes a teddy jacket warm depends more on its material and makeup than the texture of the exterior fluff. Teddy jackets with no internal lining don’t put up a great fight against the wind. That’s why the combination of teddy with something more airtight makes for the warmest of jackets. Think genuine leather plus teddy, where the leather works to block out any air and seal in your body warmth. Another great option is teddy with goose down, where the exterior fur covers a thick cushion filled with goose down.
