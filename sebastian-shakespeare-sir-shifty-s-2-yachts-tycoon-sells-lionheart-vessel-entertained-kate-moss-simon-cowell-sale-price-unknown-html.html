Sir Philip Green sells yacht where he entertained Kate Moss and Simon Cowell

Sir Philip Green with his wife Tina in Monaco

Since roly-poly retailer Sir Philip Green offered to ‘sort’ the £571 million black hole in BHS’s pension scheme, there has been much speculation about if and when he would indeed get his chequebook out.

Now the blustering billionaire has no excuse. For I can disclose that Sir Shifty has flogged the superyacht Lionheart on which he used to entertain model Kate Moss and pop mogul Simon Cowell.

The sale price is not known, but Green reportedly paid $50 million to buy the 208-foot gin palace ten years ago. ‘The yacht has been sold and the new owner changed the name,’ a broker confirms.

The three-storey vessel, which sleeps up to 12 people and has a crew of 18, has now been registered under the name Lioness V. Sir Philip declines to comment, but the new owners are believed to be from the UK and the home port of the vessel, which sails under a British ensign, is Douglas on the Isle of Man.

Its new owners use the superyacht privately and are also chartering it to plutocrats for up to £563,000 a week in high season. Marketing literature boasts of the boat’s ‘show-stopping’ pool area, ‘lavish cinema’ and ‘several options for dining’.

The tycoon was dubbed ‘Philip Greed’ after he and his family banked more than £400 million in dividends from BHS during his 15 years at the helm, before selling the High Street chain for £1 in 2015 to serial bankrupt Dominic Chappell.

If Green, who was accused of ‘cowboy capitalism’, needs to raise any more cash to support the 20,000 members of the BHS pension scheme, he could always sell his two other yachts. 

Sir Shifty has flogged the superyacht Lionheart (pictured) on which he used to entertain model Kate Moss and pop mogul Simon Cowell

The three-storey vessel, which sleeps up to 12 people and has a crew of 18, has now been registered under the name Lioness V

He and his Monte Carlo-based wife, Tina, took delivery of the newest just last month. Also named Lionheart, the £100 million monster is the length of a football pitch and has a helipad on its upper deck.

The couple, whose fortune is estimated at £3.5 billion, also own the 109-foot superyacht Lionchase, which is used mostly by their two grown-up children, Chloe and Brandon.

It’s enough to make Green’s former BHS workers feel rather seasick.

 

Don't mention the war! Berlin honours Cleese

It’s lucky that John Cleese has put that terrible business with the Germans well behind him.

The goose-stepping Fawlty Towers star, whose hotel-owning character Basil memorably urged ‘don’t mention the war’, will receive the European Broadcasting Union’s Rose d’Or Award for Lifetime Achievement in the German capital next month.

Fawlty Towers star John Cleese (pictured) will receive the European Broadcasting Union’s Rose d’Or Award for Lifetime Achievement in the German capital next month

‘I am delighted by this chance to annoy Terry Gilliam and I’m also very humbled by the offer of an all-expenses-paid holiday in Berlin,’ admits Cleese, who declined a peerage on the basis that he didn’t want to spend winters in Britain.

Presumably he’ll be afforded better hospitality than Fawlty Towers granted its Teutonic guests.

 

What would Jesus wear? 

Fashion might be seen as a world of airheads and rag-trade tycoons making fortunes from Chinese sweatshops, but staunch Christian Simon Ward is challenging stereotypes. 

The former British Fashion Council boss has written a book in which he considers what Jesus would wear today and calls for more Christians to work in the business. 

Ward admits in The Character Of Fashion that God would have ‘very different priorities’ for the business.

If you're invited for tea with The Apprentice’s Karren Brady, don’t ask for a nibble of her macaroons. 

‘I rarely indulge in sweet things, but when I do, I do not like to share,’ declares Baroness Brady of Knightsbridge. ‘Not a mouthful, or a “taste”, not a single bloody morsel. The problem with sharing is you never feel you’ve had the full portion, so end up having another. If you want some, get your own!’ 

Talk about having your cake and eating it.
