Alyssa Milano breaks silence on controversial GoFundMe fundraiser for son's baseball trip as she say

Alyssa Milano has broken her silence about her GoFundMe criticism.

The multi-millionaire star was slammed for pushing her fundraiser for her son's baseball team trip. She asked for $10K and donations are now over $13K with over 400 people donating.

The actress, 51, defended her actions after social media users flooded her Instagram page with backlash and left 'horrid' comments on her 12-year-old son Milo's account.

She shared two screenshots of him responding to trolls, reminding them that he is only a pre-teen child, and asked people to leave her children out of it.

She added that she 'cannot afford to' pay for the entire team and their family as much as she hopes to be able to do that 'maybe someday.'

Earlier this week, she was spotted driving her $200k electric Porsche Taycan, seemingly unfazed by the ongoing trolling, during an outing with her son. 

Multi-millionaire Alyssa Milano responded to the criticism she received after creating a GoFundMe fundraiser for her son's baseball team trip. She asked for $10K and donations are over $13K as of Friday; seen in December

Despite being a multimillionaire, she said she is 'no different' than other parents. 

'Every parent raises money for their child's sports teams and many of them do so through GoFund Me,' she began in her lengthy caption. 

'As much as I'd love to pay for the entire team and their families for travel, transportation, hotel, food and beverage, uniforms, trading pins and all the things teams do for this kind of trip — I cannot afford to do so,' she continued. 'Maybe someday.'

She also said that no matter what she does, it would be a lose-lose situation anyway. 

'Also, if I did pay for everyone — my trolls would find something else to be hurtful about,' she added. 

'Regardless of how you feel about me, going on to my hardworking 12 year old son's Instagram page and leaving these kinds of messages is so horrid,' she said. 

'Leave the kids alone,' she continued as she defended her son. 'Let them play baseball.'

Finally, she shared her fundraiser again and wrote: 'If you'd like to donate to help the team's families — we appreciate it — the link is in his bio'.

As of time of publication, their fundraiser has received over $13K in donations after surpassing the $10K fundraising target nearly a week ago.

The Melrose Place star set up the online fundraiser under her married name Alyssa Bugliari over a week ago.

On January 25, she shared it with her 3.4 million followers on X to ask for donations. 

Some of the backlash she received went to her son Milo's page instead and he responded to a few, writing that Milano is 'the greatest human of all time'. 

'You do realize I'm only 12 and I love my mom,' he had written in response to harsh comments on his page. 'My mom is the greatest human of all time. She does everything for everyone.' 

Under another angry comment, he wrote: 'Please get off my page. I'm only 12.' 

It's noteworthy that in his bio, it states that his Instagram account is ran by his parents.
