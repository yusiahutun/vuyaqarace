Who is Beth Thomas?

Beth Thomas is a professional nurse and author who seldom comes into the limelight. She was famous in childhood for problematic behaviour, which earned her the nickname Child of Rage.

She hit the headlines in the 80s and early 90s after terrorising her family. She is a survivor of reactive attachment disorder (RAD).

Seeking professional help

Initially, Tim and Julie Tennent did not know the cause of their daughter's rage. They tried asking the adoption agency for information, but their requests were rejected due to confidentiality laws.

The couple decided to seek professional assistance. Beth's case was handled by Dr. Ken Magid, a clinical psychologist, and later Connell Watkins, an educated therapist. She was about six when she started taking therapy sessions.

Dr. Ken Magid diagnosed her with reactive attachment disorder (RAD) and ordered a temporary separation from her adoptive family. Beth was placed in the care of an expert in raising children with RAD.

Common symptoms of RAD are self-harm, destructive behaviour, and difficulty getting along with other people. Beth was taken to a controlled environment and was placed under Connell Watkins to reverse the condition.

Through therapy, she was able to develop compassion and empathy. To help her recover from the early childhood horrors, Watkins welcomed Beth into her home and focused on building trust.

She was even taken to public school and allowed to participate in church activities. After about one year, she stopped self-harming and started showing remorse for how she treated Jonathan.

She was monitored throughout her life to make sure she did not have a RAD relapse. At some point, she was adopted by Nancy.

Child of Rage: the documentary

Beth Thomas' RAD experience inspired the 1992 documentary Child of Rage. The documentary is based on her real-life story. It tells the story of Jill Tyler and her minister husband, Rob Tyler, who adopt two children, Catherine and Eric.

It featured videos recorded during her therapy sessions with the professionals. The documentary demonstrated the dire consequences of severe neglect and sexual abuse of children.

Where is Beth Thomas now?

After receiving treatment and gaining the ability to love normally, Beth resumed school and other normal activities.

Where is Beth Thomas today? She is a nursing graduate. She is a nurse in an American healthcare facility in Arizona. She works in a neonatal ICU.

Besides being a nurse, she is an author. She and Nancy, her second adoptive mother, co-authored More Than a Thread of Hope. The book narrates the treatment and struggles Beth endured during her intensive therapy to modify her psychopathic behaviour.

She is also the founder of Families By Design. The organisation helps families whose children have been diagnosed with RAD.
