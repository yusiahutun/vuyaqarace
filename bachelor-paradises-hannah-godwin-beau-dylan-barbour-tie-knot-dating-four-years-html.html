Bachelor In Paradise's Hannah Godwin and beau Dylan Barbour tie the knot after dating four years

Hannah Godwin of Bachelor In Paradise fame tied the knot with fiancé Dylan Barbour on Wednesday, August 23, in Paris.

Sending Godwin, 28, and Barbour, 29, well wishes, Jason Oppenheim shared a clip from the festivities on Instagram.

Over an outdoor scene of fireworks, a sprawling landscape, and the newlyweds sharing a kiss, the 46-year-old real estate broker wrote, 'Congrats to Dylan & Hannah on the most beautiful Parisian wedding ever!!!'

The frame of the new husband and wife showed Hannah in a stunning white gown as she held up a glass of champagne.

Godwin and Barbour met while filming season 6 of the long-running ABC dating series.

Their season premiered in August 2019, and Godwin initially gravitated toward Blake Horstmann.

In a twist of direction, Hannah eventually got engaged to Dylan during the show’s season finale.

Post-show, the couple had a long distance relationship, with the blonde beauty living in Los Angeles while the tech entrepreneur called San Diego home. 

In March 2020, however, the pair was able to quarantine together in the wake of the coronavirus pandemic.

That year, Hannah told Us Magazine, 'Luckily, we’re both easygoing so being trapped in sweats all day with my BFF isn’t a bad day.' 

And Dylan noted, 'It makes us want to live together and puppy fever is through the roof.'

Savoring the unusual stretch of downtime, Godwin added: 'It’s rare in our hectic lives to have so much of time together, so it’s nice to focus on each other right now.'

By November 2020 the reality TV duo had officially settled in together in a house in San Diego.

Rehearsal: One of the wedding guests shared outtakes from the couple's rehearsal dinner

Excited: Godwin took to her Instagram Stories to gush over her 'garden party' rehearsal dinner setup

One week before they met at the altar, Hannah - who hails from Alabama - shared that her then-fiancé had proposed to her a second time.

Sharing a professionally shot photo of him down on one knee, in front of an ocean view, she added an explanatory caption.

'Although our first engagement was and still is SO special to us, over the past 4 years we’ve talked about having a special little moment just for us in the real world, and @dylanbarbour surprised me and made it happen last week.'

Interacting with her 1.5 million followers, she added, 'Now off to our wedding location today to tie the knot!!!'
