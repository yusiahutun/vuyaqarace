Meaning of Who Made Who [ac/dc Live] by AC/DC

In the intriguing world of rock 'n' roll, there are few bands as iconic as AC/DC. Known for their high-energy performances and hard-hitting sound, one of their standout hits, "Who Made Who [ac/dc Live]," has captured the attention of fans worldwide. This song, released in 1986, has a catchy beat and powerful lyrics that are open to interpretation. Let's dive into the depths of this classic anthem and explore its underlying themes.At its core, "Who Made Who" seems to explore the concept of power and control in the modern world. The lyrics suggest that there are external forces at play, exerting influence over individuals. It's a game, a one-on-one battle where the protagonist seems set to win every time. The first verse sets the tone, with phrases like "Face it on a level," emphasizing the challenge presented. The idea of something "owning you through and through" suggests a lack of control, a feeling that is further explored throughout the song.One standout lyric is "The databank knows my number, says I got to pay 'cause I made the grade last year." This line suggests a sense of surveillance and accountability. It implies that past actions have consequences and that the protagonist is being tracked, presumably for a debt that needs to be repaid.
